I'm Luqman Inamdar. I was 13 when I built my first real webpage from my home in Goa. I am a young web creator from Goa who loves turning ideas into real sites. At age 13, I never thought I could ship something people could visit online. Now this portfolio proves I can.

Why I Started Building Websites at 13 in Goa

I got curious after watching my cousin fix a school website. I wanted my own corner of the internet too. Goa has amazing beaches, and one evening near the beach I pictured a site that felt bright, calm, and alive. That moment pushed me to start building websites for real. I did not have fancy tools. I just had time, Wi-Fi, and curiosity.

My First Web Projects

My first project was a simple page about my favorite mangoes from Goa. It looked rough, but it was mine. Next I made a small games list site for friends. Every bug taught me something new. I kept improving because each site felt closer to what I imagined. My friends even started asking me for help with their pages.

What I'm Learning as a Young Coder

I study HTML, CSS and JavaScript every week. I break layouts, fix them, and try again. At age 13, I'm still learning, but I don't wait for perfect. I practice by building websites for hobbies, family, and school projects. Being a young web creator from Goa keeps me motivated — local shops need good sites too. HTML, CSS and JavaScript are my main tools right now. I also watch tutorials and copy code until I understand it.

This portfolio is my proof that age 13 is not too early to start. I built every page here myself. I want to keep growing, help people online, and ship cooler projects soon. I am proud, but I know I have lots to learn.
